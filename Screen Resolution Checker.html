Why Simulate Different Screen Resolutions?

Testing your website or application at different screen resolutions is crucial for:

  • Responsive Design: Ensure your layout adapts properly to all screen sizes
  • User Experience: Verify content remains readable and accessible on smaller screens
  • Development: Catch rendering issues before they affect real users
  • Quality Assurance: Test across the range of devices your audience uses

How the Simulation Works

Our simulator creates a viewport at your specified dimensions while maintaining the actual device characteristics (like pixel ratio and color depth). This gives you a more accurate representation than simply resizing your browser window.

Common Screen Resolutions for Testing

When testing responsive designs, consider these common breakpoints:

Desktop/Laptop

  • 2560×1440 (QHD/WQHD)
  • 1920×1080 (Full HD)
  • 1600×900 (HD+)
  • 1440×900 (WXGA+)
  • 1366×768 (common laptop)

Tablets

  • 1024×1366 (iPad Pro portrait)
  • 768×1024 (iPad portrait)
  • 800×1280 (common Android tablet)

Smartphones

  • 414×896 (iPhone 11/Xr)
  • 375×812 (iPhone X/XS/11 Pro)
  • 360×800 (common Android)
  • 320×568 (iPhone SE/5s)
